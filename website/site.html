Unlocking Pakistan IT Potential

Pakistan First-Ever IT and ITeS Export Strategy Launched By The Federal Minister of IT & Telecom, Dr. Umar Saif. We are pleased to announce the launching of National IT Strategy research report led by Pakistan Software Export Board (PSEB) under the direction of Federal Ministry of IT & Telecom. The study was conducted in collaboration with a consortium comprising of A.F. Fergusons & Co., PWC UK, Oxon Global, and Oxford Global Projects. The report focuses on the potential of Pakistan's IT/ITeS industry and provides a roadmap with the aim of increasing IT exports of Pakistan. This roadmap is closely aligned with the vision of our Federal Minister of IT and Telecom to reach our national target of over $10 billion dollars per year in the next 3 years (short term) and $15 billion per year in the next 5 years.

Software Technology Parks

Pakistan Software Export Board (PSEB) has presently established 28 STPs in Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad, and the secondary cities of Pakistan with a total area of 1,343,893 square feet. These STPs have been set up by converting public /private owned office buildings/ factories/ warehouses to provide tech-enabled office space to the IT & ITeS companies at affordable rates. The development of more STPs is in the pipeline to further augment the ICT industry’s growth.

Freelancers

The freelancing industry in Pakistan is growing at an escalating pace and is deemed as one of the top freelance markets by Payoneer’s Global Gig Economy Index. Ranked as the ‘Fourth Fastest-Growing Freelancing Country’ in the world with 47% revenue growth, Pakistani freelancers generate revenue of over $500 million every year. Owing to this stellar performance, the Government of Pakistan is working on the maximization of freelancers in the country.
